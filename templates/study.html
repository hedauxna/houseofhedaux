The Study
Where the journey unfolds

Step deeper into the House of Hedaux. This is the Study—a chamber of quiet ambition and earned wisdom. Here, the story of Nathan’s working life unfolds across time: from dreams of flight to the grounded joy of fatherhood, from casino nights to factory days, and finally, to the luminous world of IT where passion and purpose align.

The Study is more than a résumé. It’s a testament to adaptability, grit, and the kind of success that isn’t measured in titles, but in transformation. Each entry is a page from a life reimagined—proof that even detours can lead to destiny.

This room invites you to trace the arc of a career built not just on skill, but on love, sacrifice, and the quiet thrill of finding where you truly belong.

I didn’t take the straight path. I took the one with turbulence, detours, and unexpected beauty.

When I left school, I stepped into the world of aviation—Southend Airport, a flying school, and a dream to become an airline pilot. But life had other plans. The theory was tough, the finances tighter, and circumstances shifted. That dream slipped through my fingers… and handed me something far greater. Had I taken flight, I might never have met my wife, never held my four children in my arms. Fate, it seems, knows how to reroute us toward what truly matters.

From the skies to the casino floor, I dealt cards and met my soulmate. Together, we left Essex behind and followed my parents to Lincolnshire, seeking a safer, steadier life for our growing family.

Then came the factory years—Bakkavor, once Geest. I climbed the ranks, learned the rhythm of fresh food manufacturing, and spent 18 years as a business planner. Supply chain, commercial, finance—I absorbed it all. But behind the spreadsheets and shift patterns, a quiet passion stirred: IT. A lifelong hobby, waiting patiently in the wings.

Now, I work in tech. And I’ve never felt more at home.

The Study is where I archive this journey. Not just the roles I’ve held, but the lessons they taught me. It’s a room built on resilience, gratitude, and the thrill of finding purpose in unexpected places.
